SUBJECT: Seat Rails and Roller Housing Inspections (ATA Code: 51)

================================================================================
BACKGROUND
================================================================================

Multiple fatal accidents in Cessna singles have been attributed to pilot seat
slippage on takeoff and go-around. When a pilot's seat unexpectedly slides aft
during the takeoff roll or initial climb, the pilot can no longer reach the
rudder pedals and forward yoke input fully. This results in loss of control
at the most critical phase of flight.

Root causes include:
  - Debris (dirt, gravel) blocking the seat track locking holes, preventing
    the spring-loaded lock pin from fully engaging
  - Worn seat rail holes enlarged beyond specified diameter, reducing pin engagement
  - Bent, worn, or corroded roller housings reducing proper seat engagement
  - Weakened lock pin springs failing to maintain positive engagement force

AD 87-20-03 R2 required a one-time inspection and lubrication. Post-compliance
accidents continued; investigation revealed that lubricating the seat tracks
attracts debris, worsening the problem. This AD:
  1. Establishes recurring inspection intervals
  2. Explicitly prohibits lubricating seat rails (previous AD permitted it)
  3. Adds specific dimensional criteria for rail hole diameter and roller housing

================================================================================
COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

COMPLIANCE INTERVAL:
Inspect within the first to occur of:
  - 100 hours TIS after June 17, 2011 (initial), OR
  - 12 calendar months after June 17, 2011 (initial)

REPETITIVE INTERVAL: Thereafter at intervals not to exceed:
  - 100 hours TIS, OR
  - 12 calendar months
  whichever occurs first.

REQUIRED INSPECTIONS (perform on ALL pilot and copilot seat tracks):

  STEP 1 — VISUAL INSPECTION FOR DEBRIS:
    Remove the seat. Visually inspect each seat locking hole in the rail for
    dirt, debris, or obstruction. If found, clean before further flight.
    DO NOT LUBRICATE THE SEAT RAILS.

  STEP 2 — SEAT RAIL HOLE DIAMETER:
    Measure each seat locking pin engagement hole in the rail.
    LIMIT: If hole diameter is 0.42 inch or greater — replace rail before
    further flight.

  STEP 3 — ROLLER INSPECTION:
    Inspect each seat roller for:
      a. Flat spots on roller surface (visual/tactile)
      b. Binding or resistance in track
      If flat spots or binding found: clean (dry) or replace. DO NOT lubricate.

  STEP 4 — ROLLER HOUSING TANG THICKNESS:
    Inspect tang thickness on roller housing.
    LIMIT: If tang thickness is less than 0.050 inch — replace roller housing
    before further flight.

  STEP 5 — ROLLER HOUSING TANG LENGTH:
    Inspect tang length from inner edge to outer bend.
    LIMIT: If tang length is less than 0.230 inch — replace roller housing
    before further flight.

  STEP 6 — LOCK PIN SPRING:
    Check lock pin spring for positive, firm engagement action.
    Actuate seat adjustment lever and verify pin extends and retracts positively.
    Replace spring if weak, bent, or does not return pin promptly.

  STEP 7 — SEAT RAIL CRACK INSPECTION:
    Visually inspect seat rails for cracks per the crack criteria in Cessna
    Service Kit SK172-82 or Cessna Maintenance Manual Chapter 25.

  STEP 8 — REINSTALL AND VERIFY ENGAGEMENT DEPTH:
    Reinstall seat. Verify seat lock pin engages to minimum 0.150 inch depth.
    If engagement depth is less than 0.150 inch: repair or replace components
    to achieve minimum engagement before further flight.

  IMPORTANT: DO NOT lubricate seat rails at any step. Lubrication attracts
  debris which prevents pin engagement. Dry cleaning only (compressed air,
  lint-free cloth).

================================================================================
COST ESTIMATE
================================================================================

Labor: ~1 hour per airplane (~$85 at typical labor rates)
Rail replacement (if required): ~$395 per rail
Roller housing kit (if required): ~$280
Lock pin spring: ~$12 each

================================================================================
COMPLIANCE DOCUMENTATION
================================================================================

Each compliance entry must include:
  1. Inspection results for ALL steps (1–8) — pass/fail for each
  2. Measurements for rail hole diameter and tang dimensions
  3. Parts replaced (if any) with part numbers
  4. AD number (2011-10-09), compliance date, TIS at service, hobbs/tach
  5. Mechanic signature, certificate number

================================================================================
NOTES FOR DESERT SKY AVIATION OPERATORS
================================================================================

  • Each Cessna 172N has two front adjustable seats on floor rails; both sides
    must be inspected per this AD. Rear seats are fixed (no tracks) and are not
    subject to this AD.
  • 1978 airframes may have high total time; pay particular attention to seat rail
    hole diameter, roller condition, and lock pin engagement per the AD steps.
  • Do not lubricate seat rails — dry cleaning only, per AD.
