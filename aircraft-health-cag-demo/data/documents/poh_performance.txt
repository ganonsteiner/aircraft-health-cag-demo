CESSNA MODEL 172N SKYHAWK
PILOT'S OPERATING HANDBOOK — SECTION 5: PERFORMANCE
1978 Edition (FAA Approved)

Aircraft: N4798E  Serial: 17270798  Engine: Lycoming O-320-H2AD

All performance data is FAA-approved and reflects a standard aircraft at gross
weight (2300 lbs) in standard atmospheric conditions (ISA: 15°C, 29.92 in. Hg)
unless otherwise noted. Actual performance varies with aircraft weight, density
altitude, and non-standard temperature. Phoenix (KPHX) density altitude at field
elevation 1,135 ft MSL can exceed 5,000 ft in summer — compute density altitude
before every flight.

================================================================================
TAKEOFF PERFORMANCE
================================================================================

SHORT FIELD TAKEOFF (Flaps 10°):
  Ground roll (sea level, std day, gross):     890 feet
  Distance over 50 ft obstacle:               1,625 feet

NORMAL TAKEOFF (Flaps 0°):
  Ground roll (sea level, std day, gross):    ~960 feet (estimated; POH lists
                                              short field as primary data)

Effect of Altitude (approximate):
  At 2,500 ft density altitude:               +15% ground roll
  At 5,000 ft density altitude:               +35% ground roll
  At 7,000 ft density altitude:               +55% ground roll

NOTE: Phoenix Sky Harbor (KPHX) field elevation 1,135 ft MSL. On a summer day at
40°C (104°F), density altitude at KPHX can be 5,500–6,000 ft. Takeoff distance
can approach 1,400 ft ground roll and 2,500 ft over 50 ft obstacle.

Rotation Speed:  55 KIAS (Vr)
Lift-off Speed:  60 KIAS
Best Angle Clb:  60 KIAS (Vx) — obstacles only
Best Rate Clmb:  73 KIAS (Vy) — normal climb

================================================================================
CLIMB PERFORMANCE
================================================================================

At Sea Level, Gross Weight, Full Power:
  Rate of Climb:    770 fpm
  Calibrated AS:    73 KIAS (Vy)

Density Altitude vs. Rate of Climb:
  At 4,000 ft DA:   ~580 fpm
  At 8,000 ft DA:   ~360 fpm
  At 12,000 ft DA:  ~120 fpm

Service Ceiling:   14,200 ft (100 fpm climb rate)
Absolute Ceiling:  ~15,000 ft (0 fpm)

Cruise Climb:      80–85 KIAS (recommended for engine cooling; lower CHT than Vy)

================================================================================
CRUISE PERFORMANCE
================================================================================

Altimeter:    8,000 ft MSL (standard IFR cruise altitude for Southwest routes)

  75% Power (2450 RPM):     122 KTAS     ~9.7 gph     range ~520 nm (43 gal usable)
  65% Power (2350 RPM):     114 KTAS     ~8.4 gph     range ~590 nm
  55% Power (2200 RPM):     105 KTAS     ~7.5 gph     range ~640 nm

At 4,000 ft MSL (common VFR cruise altitude):
  75% Power:                115 KTAS     ~9.5 gph
  65% Power:                108 KTAS     ~8.2 gph

Economy Cruise (55% power): Recommended for maximum engine longevity and lowest
  CHT/EGT — particularly important for H2AD variant.

FUEL FLOW AT CRUISE:
  Full rich:         11–12 gph (all altitudes)
  Best power:         9.5–10.5 gph (50° ROP)
  Best economy:       7.5–8.5 gph (peak EGT or 50° LOP)
  Lean of peak (LOP): 7.0–7.5 gph (advanced leaning — verify engine is approved)

================================================================================
RANGE AND ENDURANCE
================================================================================

  Usable Fuel: 43 gallons

  Range (no reserves):
    75% power at 8,000 ft:    ~520 nm (4.3 hours)
    65% power at 8,000 ft:    ~590 nm (5.2 hours)
    55% power at 8,000 ft:    ~650 nm (6.2 hours)

  FAR 91 Day VFR Reserve:    30 minutes at cruise power (add ~50 nm to required fuel)
  FAR 91 IFR Reserve:        45 minutes at cruise power

  Practical Mission Range (with reserves, 45-min fuel):
    Short hop KPHX–KSDL:      15 nm (15 min; trivial fuel)
    KPHX–KPRC:                85 nm (45 min at 115 KTAS)
    KPHX–KTUS:               100 nm (50 min)
    KPHX–KGCN:               200 nm (100 min) — easily within range
    KPHX–KLAS:               250 nm (130 min) — doable with reserve planning

================================================================================
LANDING PERFORMANCE
================================================================================

SHORT FIELD LANDING (Full flaps, 61 KIAS over threshold):
  Ground roll:          520 feet
  Distance over 50 ft obstacle: 1,280 feet

NORMAL LANDING (Flaps 20°–30°):
  Approach speed:       65–70 KIAS
  Ground roll:          ~600 feet

Effect of Altitude:
  At 5,000 ft density altitude: landing distance increases ~25%
  (air is less dense; true airspeed higher at same IAS)

Effect of Wind:
  Headwind: reduces landing distance significantly
  Tailwind: INCREASES landing distance — limit tailwind to 10 knots max

================================================================================
GLIDE PERFORMANCE (ENGINE INOPERATIVE)
================================================================================

Best Glide Speed:    65 KIAS (flaps up)
Glide Ratio:         ~9:1 (approximately)

Distance from altitude:
  From 1,000 ft AGL:   ~1.5 nm glide distance
  From 5,000 ft AGL:   ~7.5 nm
  From 8,000 ft AGL:   ~12 nm

Phoenix Departure Profile: After typical KPHX departure, reach 6,000 ft MSL
  (4,800 ft AGL) within ~10 nm. From that point, ~7–8 nm glide range.
  Phoenix metro area (extensive flat desert/urban) provides abundant emergency
  landing options at any point along typical departure routes.

================================================================================
DENSITY ALTITUDE EFFECTS (PHOENIX — KPHX SPECIFIC)
================================================================================

KPHX Field Elevation: 1,135 ft MSL

Summer Operations (June–September):
  Standard temp at 1,135 ft: ~18°C
  Phoenix summer temp:       38–45°C
  
  Density Altitude at 40°C:  ~5,000 ft
  Density Altitude at 45°C:  ~5,600 ft

  At 5,000 ft density altitude:
    Takeoff ground roll:      ~1,200 ft (vs. 890 ft sea level)
    Rate of climb:            ~450–550 fpm (vs. 770 fpm sea level)
    Cruise speed penalty:     ~5 KTAS

CAUTION: Morning or evening departures strongly recommended in summer.
  Midday DA at KPHX in July routinely exceeds 5,500 ft.
  Full gross weight operations should be avoided in extreme heat.
