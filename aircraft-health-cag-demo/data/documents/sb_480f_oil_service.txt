LYCOMING ENGINES
SERVICE BULLETIN

SB Number:    480F (supersedes 480E, 480D, 480C, 480B, 480A, 480)
Date Issued:  May 25, 2017
Classification: MANDATORY
Subject:      Oil Servicing — All Direct-Drive Lycoming Engines

Applicability: All Lycoming direct-drive aircraft engines including:
               O-320, IO-320, AIO-320, LIO-320
               O-360, HO-360, IO-360, AIO-360, HIO-360, TIO-360
               O-540, IO-540, TIO-540, LTIO-540
               And all other direct-drive Lycoming models
               INCLUDING O-320-H2AD (N4798E engine)

================================================================================
PURPOSE
================================================================================

This service bulletin establishes oil change intervals and oil servicing
procedures to maintain proper engine lubrication and early detection of
internal wear through metallic particle inspection. Compliance with this
bulletin, combined with AD 80-04-03 R2 for H series engines, is the primary
defense against undetected internal wear.

================================================================================
OIL CHANGE INTERVALS
================================================================================

NEW OR RECENTLY OVERHAULED ENGINE:
  First oil change: Within 25 hours TIS or 4 calendar months after initial
  run-in, whichever occurs first.
  
  Note: New and overhauled engines have a "run-in" period where manufacturing
  residue, initial wear particles, and assembly compounds are present in the oil.
  Early oil changes remove this contamination and establish baseline for subsequent
  oil analysis.

ESTABLISHED ENGINE (after first oil change):
  With full-flow oil filter installed:
    Every 50 hours TIS OR 4 calendar months, whichever occurs first.
  
  Without full-flow filter (screen only):
    Every 25 hours TIS OR 4 calendar months, whichever occurs first.

O-320-H2AD NOTE: The H2AD has a full-flow spin-on filter. The 50-hour/4-month
interval applies. However, per AD 80-04-03 R2, the oil filter must be CUT OPEN
and inspected at every oil change.

================================================================================
OIL TYPE AND GRADE
================================================================================

Approved Oil Types:
  1. Ashless Dispersant (AD) mineral aviation oil — preferred
     SAE grade based on OAT:
       Above 16°C (60°F):    SAE 50 or SAE 40
       0°C to 16°C:           SAE 40 or SAE 30
       Below 0°C:             SAE 30 (preheat may be needed)

  2. Multiviscosity Aviation Oil (approved per this SB):
     - AeroShell W 15W-50 (pre-additized, AMOC for AD 80-04-03 R2)
     - AeroShell W 100 Plus (pre-additized)
     - Phillips X/C 20W-50
     - Exxon Elite 20W-50 (pre-additized, AMOC for AD 80-04-03 R2)
     - Castrol Aviation Oil W 80, W 100, W 120

  NOT APPROVED: Automotive engine oil (even if same viscosity grade)
                Synthetic automotive oil
                Non-aviation mineral oil

  BREAK-IN OIL: Use straight mineral oil (non-ashless dispersant) for first
  50 hours on new or overhauled engine. This is required to allow proper ring
  seating. Switch to AD oil after 50-hour initial oil change.

================================================================================
OIL FILTER AND SCREEN INSPECTION
================================================================================

AT EACH OIL CHANGE — MANDATORY:

  STEP 1 — DRAIN OIL:
    Drain oil with engine warm (better particle suspension).
    Allow full drain; sump plug safety-wired after installation.

  STEP 2 — REMOVE AND CUT OPEN OIL FILTER:
    Using an oil filter cutter tool, cut the full-flow filter element.
    Spread the filter media and inspect for metallic particles.
    Use a magnet (rare earth magnet preferred) to separate:
      FERROUS particles (magnetic): iron, steel — from gears, cam, lifters,
                                    crankshaft components
      NON-FERROUS particles (non-magnetic): bronze, aluminum, lead —
                                    from bearings, bushings

  STEP 3 — INSPECT OIL SUCTION SCREEN:
    Remove the engine oil sump suction screen.
    Inspect for metallic particles and debris.
    Clean screen with solvent; blow dry with compressed air.
    Reinstall with new gasket if required.

  STEP 4 — PARTICLE ASSESSMENT TABLE:

    NO SIGNIFICANT PARTICLES:
      Fine gray metallic "paste" on magnet (normal wear products):
        — Normal. Document as "no significant metal." Continue operation.
      
      Fine non-ferrous dust:
        — Normal bearing material. Document. Monitor trend.

    FERROUS PARTICLES (steel/iron chips, flakes, or fragments):
      Quantity: Any recognizable chip or flake >1mm:
        — GROUND AIRCRAFT. Perform internal inspection:
          a. Remove rocker covers; inspect rockers, push rod tips, lifters
          b. Remove oil sump screen: look for ferrous debris accumulation
          c. Inspect accessible cam lobes via pushrod tubes (if possible)
          d. If source not identified: engine teardown required
        — For O-320-H series: Specifically inspect cam lobes and all 8 lifters
          per AD 80-04-03 R2 requirements.

    LARGE METAL PARTICLES (any material, >3mm):
      — GROUND AIRCRAFT immediately. Internal inspection required before flight.
      — Consult Lycoming Service Center.

    BEARING MATERIAL (bronze material, significant quantity):
      — Consult Lycoming Service Center regarding bearing replacement.

================================================================================
OIL ANALYSIS (RECOMMENDED)
================================================================================

Lycoming recommends periodic spectrographic oil analysis (send-out sample):
  Recommended: Every other oil change (every 100 hours)
  Send to: Blackstone Laboratories, Oil Analyzers Inc., or equivalent
  Report provides: Parts-per-million of wear metals — trend analysis is more
                   valuable than single samples. Establish baseline after overhaul.

Key wear metals to watch (O-320-H2AD specific):
  Iron (Fe): Cam lobes, lifters, crankshaft, cylinder walls, rings
  Chromium (Cr): Piston rings (chrome-faced)
  Copper (Cu): Wrist pin bushings, thrust washers
  Lead (Pb): Main and rod bearings (lead-tin alloy)
  Aluminum (Al): Pistons, engine case
  Silicon (Si): Dirt ingestion (air filter condition indicator)

================================================================================
NOTES FOR N4798E (O-320-H2AD)
================================================================================

Current Engine Status (2026): ~1,450 hours SMOH
Recommended: Oil samples sent to Blackstone every other oil change
             Next oil change due: 50 hours from last or 4 months from last
             (whichever first)

At 1,450 SMOH, the engine is well into the middle third of its TBO period.
Oil analysis trends should be watched carefully for rising iron levels,
which could indicate cam/lifter wear resuming on the replacement components.

Proactive: If iron trend shows consistent rise over 3+ consecutive samples,
schedule cam/lifter borescope inspection before next oil change.
