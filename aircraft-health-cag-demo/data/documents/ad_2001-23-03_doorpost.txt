FEDERAL AVIATION ADMINISTRATION
AIRWORTHINESS DIRECTIVE

AD Number:    2001-23-03
Amendment:    39-12500
Docket:       2000-CE-26-AD
Supersedes:   AD 80-04-08

APPLICABILITY: Cessna Aircraft Company Models 172N, 172P, R172K, 172RG,
               F172N, F172P, FR172J, and FR172K airplanes.

               Cessna 172N serial numbers: 17267585 through 17274009
               (plus specific additional S/Ns: 17261445, 17261578, 17270050)

               N4798E Serial 17270798 IS within the affected range.

EFFECTIVE DATE: December 27, 2001

SUBJECT: Forward Door Post Map Light Switch and Fuel Line

REFERENCE: Cessna Service Bulletin SEB00-1, dated January 17, 2000

================================================================================
BACKGROUND
================================================================================

The left forward door post (A-pillar) on affected Cessna 172 variants contains
both a map light switch (P/N 0511080-1) and an adjacent fuel line. The switch
uses a two-hole plastic insulator over its terminals to prevent contact with
adjacent structure and wiring.

In service, the plastic insulator deteriorates from heat and UV exposure. As the
insulator degrades, its mounting holes elongate, allowing the switch body and
exposed terminals to contact the nearby fuel line. When the switch is actuated,
the resulting electrical arc can burn through the fuel line, spraying fuel into
the cabin door post area and potentially igniting a fire.

AD 80-04-08 required a one-time inspection. Post-compliance incidents continued
occurring as insulators degraded over time. This revised AD establishes a
recurring annual inspection to catch deterioration before it poses a fire hazard.

================================================================================
COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

INITIAL COMPLIANCE:
Within the first to occur of:
  - 100 hours time-in-service (TIS) after December 27, 2001, OR
  - 12 calendar months after December 27, 2001

REPETITIVE COMPLIANCE:
Thereafter, at intervals not to exceed 12 calendar months.

REQUIRED ACTIONS:

  1. MAP LIGHT SWITCH INSULATOR INSPECTION:
     Inspect the plastic insulator on the map light switch (P/N 0511080-1) in
     the left forward door post for the following:
       a. Presence — insulator must be installed
       b. Condition — insulator must not be cracked, brittle, elongated at
          mounting holes, or deformed

  2. IF INSULATOR IS MISSING OR UNAIRWORTHY:
     Before further flight, install a new insulator per Cessna SEB00-1.
     Verify switch cannot contact fuel line when insulator is installed.

  3. FUEL LINE INSPECTION:
     Inspect the fuel line in the left forward door post area for:
       a. Chafing, cuts, or abrasion from contact with switch or structure
       b. Evidence of heat damage or discoloration
       c. Leaking fuel

  4. IF FUEL LINE IS DAMAGED:
     Before further flight, replace the damaged fuel line section per
     Cessna Maintenance Manual and Service Bulletin SEB00-1.

================================================================================
COMPLIANCE DOCUMENTATION
================================================================================

Each annual inspection maintenance entry must include:
  1. Statement that door post map light switch insulator was inspected
  2. Condition of insulator (satisfactory / replaced)
  3. Condition of door post fuel line (satisfactory / replaced)
  4. AD number (2001-23-03), compliance date, hobbs/tach
  5. Inspector signature, certificate number, IA endorsement

================================================================================
NOTES FOR N4798E
================================================================================

N4798E is specifically within the affected serial number range (17270798).
This AD must be complied with annually. Compliance is typically accomplished
as part of the annual inspection by the inspecting IA.

Maps light switches on aircraft of this vintage often have faded or cracked
insulators. Allow extra inspection time in the door post area during annuals.
