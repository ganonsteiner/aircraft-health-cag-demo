BOEING 737-800 NG
FLIGHT CREW OPERATIONS MANUAL — CHAPTER 3: EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
Applicable to: CFM56-7B powered 737-800 (Southwest Airlines fleet)

WARNING: Accomplish emergency procedures from memory where noted (M).
         Non-normal checklist items are completed from the QRH.

================================================================================
ENGINE FAILURE DURING TAKEOFF ROLL (below V1)
================================================================================

1. Thrust levers ........................................ IDLE (M)
2. Brakes ................................................ APPLY MAX (M)
3. Speed brakes .......................................... ARM confirmed deployed (M)
4. Reverse thrust ....................................... MAX (M), as available
5. If runway remaining insufficient: field arrest or overrun procedures
6. After stopping: engine fire/fuel leak — shutdown if required (QRH)

================================================================================
ENGINE FAILURE / FIRE AFTER V1
================================================================================

1. Continue takeoff — maintain runway heading (M)
2. At positive rate: gear UP (M)
3. At acceleration altitude:
   a. Thrust lever (affected engine) ..................... CLOSE (M)
   b. Engine fuel control switch ......................... CUTOFF (M)
4. If engine fire warning persists:
   a. Engine fire switch ................................. PULL (M)
   b. After 30 seconds: rotate to ARM, then discharge (M)
5. Accomplish Non-Normal Checklist: ENGINE FIRE / ENGINE SEVERE DAMAGE (QRH)

================================================================================
ENGINE SURGE / STALL IN FLIGHT
================================================================================

1. Thrust lever (affected) .............................. REDUCE to idle
2. If surge persists: engine shutdown procedure (QRH)
3. If EGT exceedance occurred: maintenance inspection required before next flight
4. Monitor N2, N1, EGT deviations, oil pressure

================================================================================
HIGH OIL TEMPERATURE / OIL PRESSURE LOSS
================================================================================

1. Thrust lever (affected) .............................. REDUCE
2. Oil pressure gauge ................................... MONITOR
3. If oil pressure below minimum or oil temp above 155 °C:
   a. Prepare for engine shutdown (QRH ENGINE OIL PRESSURE LOW)
4. Do not exceed oil temperature limits even if pressure appears normal

================================================================================
ELEVATED EGT DEVIATION (in-flight ACARS alert or EICAS message)
================================================================================

1. Thrust lever (affected) .............................. REDUCE as required
2. Monitor EGT deviation trend via ACARS
3. If deviation exceeds +20 °C or increases >5 °C in <10 flights:
   a. Initiate descent to lower altitude as practicable
   b. Notify maintenance on arrival — borescope inspection required
4. If EGT continues to rise uncontrolled: engine shutdown procedure (QRH)

================================================================================
ENGINE FIRE — CONFIRMED IN FLIGHT
================================================================================

1. Thrust lever (affected) .............................. IDLE (M)
2. A/T arm switch (affected) ............................ OFF (M)
3. Engine fuel control switch ........................... CUTOFF (M)
4. Engine fire switch ................................... PULL (M)
5. If fire persists after 30 s: bottle discharge (alternate) (M)
6. Land at nearest suitable airport
7. After landing: fire services — do not use reverse on affected engine

================================================================================
PRECAUTIONARY / EMERGENCY LANDING
================================================================================

1. Squawk 7700 and declare emergency ATC
2. Passenger announcement — brace position if required
3. QRH: appropriate abnormal/emergency checklist
4. Fuel dump if available and time permits
5. Fly landing profile for gross weight; VREF + wind additive applies
6. After landing: maintenance inspection before next flight; file ASR/MOR

================================================================================
CABIN PRESSURE LOSS (rapid / explosive)
================================================================================

1. Oxygen masks — don (M, < 5 seconds)
2. Crew oxygen switch ................................. ON (M)
3. Initiate emergency descent (M): Mach/speed brakes, thrust IDLE
4. Target 10,000 ft or MEA, whichever is higher
5. Notify ATC: emergency descent
6. QRH: EMERGENCY DESCENT checklist

================================================================================
NOTES
================================================================================

(M) = Memory item — accomplished from memory before QRH.
All procedures are abbreviated for demo reference only.
Full crew coordination and QRH compliance required in operations.
