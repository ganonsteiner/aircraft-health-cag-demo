CESSNA MODEL 172N SKYHAWK
PILOT'S OPERATING HANDBOOK — SECTION 3: EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
1978 Edition (FAA Approved)

Type: Cessna 172N (1978) · Engine: Lycoming O-320-H2AD

WARNING: Emergency procedures in this section are the recommended procedures for
coping with the emergencies listed. It is the pilot's responsibility to be familiar
with these procedures.

================================================================================
ENGINE FAILURE DURING TAKEOFF ROLL
================================================================================

1. Throttle ..................................... IDLE
2. Brakes ....................................... APPLY FIRMLY
3. Wing Flaps ................................... RETRACT (if extended)
4. Mixture ..................................... IDLE CUTOFF
5. Ignition Switch .............................. OFF
6. Master Switch ................................ OFF

================================================================================
ENGINE FAILURE IMMEDIATELY AFTER TAKEOFF
================================================================================

1. Airspeed ..................................... 65 KIAS (flaps up)
                                                60 KIAS (flaps down)
2. Mixture ...................................... IDLE CUTOFF
3. Fuel Selector Valve .......................... OFF
4. Ignition Switch .............................. OFF
5. Wing Flaps ................................... AS REQUIRED (do not extend above 20°
                                                if time permits, extend fully for
                                                shortest landing)
6. Master Switch ................................ OFF
7. Land straight ahead; avoid turns if altitude permits

NOTE: If altitude permits, attempt restart per ENGINE FAILURE DURING FLIGHT procedure.
      With altitude below 1,000 ft AGL, land straight ahead. Do not attempt a 180.

================================================================================
ENGINE FAILURE DURING FLIGHT (RESTART ATTEMPT)
================================================================================

1. Airspeed ..................................... 65 KIAS (best glide)
   Glide ratio: approximately 9:1 (clean configuration, 65 KIAS)

2. Carburetor Heat .............................. ON (check for carb ice)
3. Fuel Selector Valve .......................... BOTH
4. Mixture ...................................... RICH
5. Primer ....................................... IN AND LOCKED
6. Ignition Switch .............................. BOTH (or L, then R)
7. Throttle ..................................... 1/4 OPEN

If engine does not restart:
8. Mixture ...................................... IDLE CUTOFF
9. Fuel Selector Valve .......................... OFF
10. Ignition Switch ............................. OFF
11. Wing Flaps .................................. AS REQUIRED
12. Master Switch ............................... OFF (retain battery for radio/lights)
13. Execute emergency landing (see EMERGENCY LANDING WITHOUT ENGINE POWER)

================================================================================
ENGINE FIRE DURING START
================================================================================

If fire is ingested into the engine:
1. Starter ...................................... CONTINUE CRANKING (to blow fire out)
2. Throttle ..................................... FULL OPEN
3. Mixture ...................................... IDLE CUTOFF

If fire persists:
4. Ignition Switch .............................. OFF
5. Master Switch ................................ OFF
6. Fuel Selector Valve .......................... OFF
7. Evacuate aircraft; use fire extinguisher

================================================================================
ENGINE FIRE IN FLIGHT
================================================================================

1. Mixture ...................................... IDLE CUTOFF
2. Fuel Selector Valve .......................... OFF
3. Master Switch ................................ OFF
4. Cabin Vents/Heat ............................. CLOSED
5. Airspeed ..................................... 100 KIAS (to blow out fire)
   — If fire extinguished: increase speed to determine if resumed glide is
     possible. Do not attempt restart.
   — If fire persists: sideslip to keep flames away from cabin.
6. Execute emergency landing

================================================================================
ELECTRICAL FIRE IN FLIGHT
================================================================================

1. Master Switch ................................ OFF
2. All other switches ........................... OFF
3. Vents/Cabin Air/Heat ......................... CLOSED
4. Fire Extinguisher ............................ ACTIVATE (if fire persists in cabin)
5. Land as soon as possible

After fire extinguished:
6. Vents ........................................ OPEN (clear fumes)
7. Master Switch ................................ ON (if essential)
8. Circuit Breakers ............................. CHECK; do not reset tripped breakers

================================================================================
CABIN FIRE
================================================================================

1. Master Switch ................................ OFF
2. Vents/Cabin Air/Heat ......................... CLOSED
3. Fire Extinguisher ............................ ACTIVATE
4. Land as soon as possible
5. After landing: open windows and doors to clear fumes before exiting

================================================================================
WING FLAP FAILURE
================================================================================

Asymmetric Flap (one flap only):
1. Throttle ..................................... AS REQUIRED
2. Airspeed ..................................... REDUCE to 85 KIAS or below
3. Use ailerons to maintain wings level
4. Land at lowest practical speed; expect higher than normal roll tendency on
   the side with extended flap.

Flap Failure (flaps stuck down):
1. Approach and land at minimum practical airspeed
2. Approach at 65 KIAS (flaps down)
3. Expect higher drag, faster descent rate

Flap Failure (flaps stuck up):
1. Approach at 65–70 KIAS
2. Landing roll will be extended — plan for longer landing distance

================================================================================
EMERGENCY LANDING WITHOUT ENGINE POWER
================================================================================

1. Airspeed ..................................... 65 KIAS (best glide, flaps up)
2. Suitable landing area ....................... SELECT
3. Carburetor Heat .............................. ON
4. Fuel Selector Valve .......................... BOTH
5. Mixture ...................................... RICH
6. Ignition Switch .............................. BOTH
7. Mayday ....................................... TRANSMIT (121.5 MHz, squawk 7700)
8. Transponder .................................. 7700 (emergency)
9. When landing assured:
   a. Wing Flaps ................................ AS REQUIRED (20°–40°)
   b. Mixture ................................... IDLE CUTOFF
   c. Fuel Selector Valve ....................... OFF
   d. Ignition Switch ........................... OFF
   e. Seat Belts/Shoulder Harnesses ............. SECURE AND TIGHT
   f. Master Switch ............................. OFF (just before touchdown)
   g. Land into wind if possible; avoid obstacles

NOTE: Best glide speed 65 KIAS gives approximately 9:1 glide ratio.
      From 8,000 ft MSL (typical cruise altitude at KPHX), glide range is
      approximately 11–13 nm. Phoenix area terrain is favorable for off-airport
      landings in open desert.

================================================================================
DITCHING (OVER WATER)
================================================================================

1. Radio/ELT .................................... TRANSMIT MAYDAY; activate ELT if time
2. Heavy Objects ................................ SECURE OR JETTISON
3. Approach: Into wind if possible, along swells if large swells present
4. Flaps ....................................... 30° (full)
5. Power ....................................... AS AVAILABLE
6. Seats ....................................... UPRIGHT AND LOCKED
7. Belts ........................................ TIGHT
8. Doors ....................................... UNLATCH (prevent jamming on impact)
9. Touch down at minimum airspeed, wings level, nose slightly high
10. Vacate aircraft immediately; life preservers on

================================================================================
INADVERTENT SPIRAL DIVE / UNUSUAL ATTITUDE RECOVERY
================================================================================

1. Throttle ..................................... IDLE
2. Wings ........................................ LEVEL with aileron/rudder
3. Pull out smoothly — do NOT pull while banked (risk of exceeding Va or Va-equivalent)
4. Trim ........................................ NEUTRAL
5. Resume normal flight

================================================================================
AMMETER / ALTERNATOR FAILURE
================================================================================

Low voltage or zero charge indication:
1. Ammeter ...................................... CHECK (should show slight positive charge)
2. ALT CB ....................................... CHECK; reset once if tripped
3. Alternator Field CB .......................... CHECK; reset once if tripped
4. If no charge:
   a. All non-essential electrical equipment . OFF
   b. Land as soon as practical
   c. Battery endurance (typical): 30 minutes with minimal load

================================================================================
LOSS OF OIL PRESSURE
================================================================================

1. If oil pressure drops below 25 psi at cruise:
   — Land as soon as practical
   — Do NOT operate engine at high power with low oil pressure
2. If oil pressure drops to zero:
   — Reduce power immediately
   — Land as soon as possible
   — Engine seizure imminent if pressure is truly zero

WARNING: O-320-H2AD engines are particularly sensitive to oil system anomalies
due to the barrel lifter design. Any oil pressure indication below 60 psi in
cruise should prompt immediate descent and precautionary landing. Consult
maintenance records for AD 80-04-03 R2 compliance and recent oil filter
inspection results.
