AIRWORTHINESS DIRECTIVE
Federal Aviation Administration
14 CFR Part 39

AD 2020-14-06: CFM International CFM56-7B Series Turbofan Engines
High-Pressure Turbine (HPT) Stage 1 Blade Inspection

SUMMARY
This AD requires repetitive borescope inspections of the HPT Stage 1 blades on CFM56-7B series engines installed on Boeing 737-700/800/900 series aircraft. Certain HPT Stage 1 blades may develop fatigue cracks in the blade platform region due to elevated thermal cycling loads.

APPLICABILITY
CFM56-7B series engines (all dash variants: -7B20, -7B22, -7B24, -7B26, -7B27, -7B27A) installed on Boeing 737 Next Generation series aircraft.

COMPLIANCE
Required as indicated, unless already accomplished:

(a) Within 500 flight cycles after the effective date of this AD, or before further flight after any hard landing (g-load > 2.1g), perform a borescope inspection of the HPT Stage 1 blades for cracking per the applicable CFM56-7B Engine Shop Manual (ESM) Task 72-00-00-200-XXX.

(b) Thereafter, repeat the inspection at intervals not to exceed 750 flight cycles.

(c) If any crack exceeding 0.5 mm is found, remove the affected blade before further flight.

ACTIONS AND COMPLIANCE
- Inspection method: Borescope (contact endoscope or equivalent)
- Access: Via HPT stage 1 borescope ports on the engine nacelle
- Blade rejection criteria: Cracks > 0.5 mm in platform region; tip rubs > 1.5 mm

COSTS OF COMPLIANCE
Estimated 1.5 work-hours per engine per inspection cycle. Parts cost applicable only upon rejection.

AUTHORITY
49 USC 106(g), 40113, 44701; 14 CFR 39.19, 39.23.

Effective Date: July 14, 2020
