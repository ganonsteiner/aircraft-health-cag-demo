AD Number:    80-04-03 R2
Amendment:    39-5893 (Revision 2; originally 39-3786 dated February 8, 1980;

DOCKET:       77-CE-44-AD

APPLICABILITY: Avco Lycoming O-320-H (all series), O-360-E, LO-360-E,
               TO-360-E, LTO-360-E series aircraft engines; all serial numbers.

EFFECTIVE DATE: April 25, 1988

SUBJECT: Hydraulic Lifter (Tappet) Spalling and Bent Push Rods

================================================================================
BACKGROUND
================================================================================

The O-320-H and O-360-E series engines use barrel-shaped (cylindrical) hydraulic
tappets and a matching wide-lobe camshaft — a design intended to allow tappet
servicing without splitting the crankcase. In service, Lycoming identified that
this configuration experiences accelerated cam lobe spalling (surface fatigue
of the cam lobe hardened surface) under normal operating loads.

Spalled cam lobe material is carried through the oil system and can cause:
  (1) Oil filter and screen clogging, reducing oil flow
  (2) Scoring of main bearings and rod bearings
  (3) Hydraulic lifter collapse (bent push rods, valve float)
  (4) Engine seizure if undetected

This AD establishes recurring mandatory inspection procedures to detect cam and
lifter deterioration before it causes engine failure.

================================================================================
COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

PART (a) — ONE-TIME COMPLIANCE (applies to O-320-H only):

For engines with serial numbers up to L-6182-76 AND engines overhauled before
March 19, 1979:

Within 50 hours time-in-service after the effective date of this AD, accomplish
the following per Lycoming Service Bulletin No. 435 (dated March 17, 1979):

  1. Replace upper exhaust valve spring seats with P/N LW-16475-KLI
  2. Replace exhaust hydraulic lifters with P/N LW-16586

NOTE: For each engine, verify whether Part (a) applies from the engine logbook
serial number (L-XXXXX-76 vintage) and overhaul history. If the engine was
overhauled after March 19, 1979, Part (a) is N/A; Part (b) is still required.

PART (b) — RECURRING COMPLIANCE:

At every engine oil change (not to exceed 100 hours time-in-service), accomplish
the following:

  1. ADD anti-wear oil additive:
     Add one 6-oz can of Lycoming anti-wear oil additive P/N LW-16702 to the
     engine oil at each oil change.

     AMOC (Alternative Means of Compliance): Per SAIB NE-06-31R2 (effective
     February 28, 2012), the following pre-additized aviation oils may be used
     in lieu of the separate additive can:
       - AeroShell W 15W-50
       - AeroShell W 100 Plus
       - Exxon Elite 20W-50
       - Castrol Aviation Oil W 80, W 100, W 120

  2. INSPECT oil suction screen:
     Remove and inspect the engine oil suction screen (sump screen) for ferrous
     metal particles. Use a magnet to distinguish ferrous (iron/steel) from
     non-ferrous (bronze/aluminum) particles.

  3. INSPECT oil filter:
     Cut open the full-flow oil filter element and inspect for metallic particles.
     Separate ferrous and non-ferrous particles.

  4. PARTICLE ASSESSMENT:

     No significant particles: Continue operation. Document results in logbook.

     Ferrous particles present (iron/steel filings, chips, or fragments):
       a. Inspect all camshaft lobes for wear, pitting, or spalling
       b. Inspect all 8 hydraulic lifters (tappets) for wear, scoring, or collapse
       c. Inspect push rods for bending
       d. If any cam lobe or lifter defect found: Replace defective parts per
          Lycoming Overhaul Manual before returning to service
       e. If engine has "T mod" (large-diameter lifters/wide-lobe cam installed
          post-March 1979): Notify FAA New York ACO in writing within 10 days
       f. Increase oil change interval to 25 hours until 3 consecutive clean filters

     Bronze/aluminum particles (connecting rod bushing, bearing material):
       a. May indicate different wear mode; consult Lycoming Service Center
       b. Document in engine logbook and monitor trend

================================================================================
COMPLIANCE DOCUMENTATION
================================================================================

Each oil change maintenance entry must document:
  1. Date and hobbs/tach at service
  2. Oil filter inspection results ("no significant metal" or describe findings)
  3. Suction screen inspection results
  4. Anti-wear additive used (P/N LW-16702 or AMOC oil type)
  5. Parts replaced (if any)
  6. Mechanic signature, certificate number
