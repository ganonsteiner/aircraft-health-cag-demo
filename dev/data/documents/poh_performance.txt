BOEING 737-800 NG
FLIGHT CREW OPERATIONS MANUAL — CHAPTER 5: PERFORMANCE
Applicable to: CFM56-7B powered 737-800 (Southwest Airlines fleet)

All data for ISA (15 °C, 29.92 in Hg) unless noted.
Actual performance varies with weight, temperature, altitude, and flap setting.
Always use QRH/QRH or AFM-approved performance software for dispatch.

================================================================================
TAKEOFF PERFORMANCE (sea level, ISA, MTOW 174,200 lbs)
================================================================================

Takeoff field length (balanced, Flaps 5):    ~8,500 ft (estimated, std day)
V1 (typical for MTOW, sea level):            ~155 KIAS (varies with OAT/field elev)
VR (typical):                                ~157 KIAS
V2 (typical):                                ~162 KIAS
Initial climb speed (gear up):               V2 + 10 to V2 + 20 KIAS

At PHX (field elevation 1,135 ft MSL) in summer (OAT +40 °C / ISA+25):
  Density altitude: ~5,000–6,000 ft
  Takeoff performance penalty: approximately 20–25% field length increase.
  Derate / assumed temperature takeoff recommended; verify with performance tool.

================================================================================
CLIMB PERFORMANCE
================================================================================

Typical climb profile (standard NADP2):
  Gear up: V2 + 10 to 1,000 ft AAL
  Flap retraction: per schedule
  Climb speed clean: 280 KIAS / M0.78 at cruise altitude

Initial climb thrust (standard day, MTOW):   2 × 27,300 lbf
Rate of climb at FL100 (MTOW):               ~2,500 fpm
Rate of climb at FL350 (mid-cruise weight):  ~1,200 fpm

================================================================================
CRUISE PERFORMANCE
================================================================================

Typical cruise altitude:   FL350–FL390 (step-climb as fuel burns off)
Long range cruise (LRC):   M0.785 (approximately 450 KTAS at FL370)
High speed cruise:         M0.82 (VMO)
Fuel flow per engine (typical cruise, FL370, M0.78, ISA):  ~2,200–2,400 lbs/hr
                           (Total approx. 4,400–4,800 lbs/hr)

================================================================================
RANGE AND ENDURANCE
================================================================================

Design range at MTOW:   ~2,900 nm (typical airline payload, NBAA IFR reserves)
Range with max payload:  ~2,060 nm
Maximum endurance:       ~7.5 hours (fuel exhaustion only; reserve not included)

Southwest Airlines typical sector: 400–1,200 nm; fuel reserves per FAR 121.

================================================================================
LANDING PERFORMANCE (typical landing weight ~135,000 lbs)
================================================================================

Approach speed (VREF + additive):
  Flaps 30:   ~137 KIAS at 135,000 lbs (VREF varies with gross weight)
  Flaps 40:   ~130 KIAS at 135,000 lbs

Landing field length (flaps 40, autobrake 3, dry):   ~5,200 ft (estimated)
Maximum demonstrated crosswind (landing):             38 kts

================================================================================
HOT AND HIGH OPERATIONS (KPHX NOTE)
================================================================================

Phoenix Sky Harbor (KPHX) is a high-density-altitude environment in summer.
Operations above 40 °C OAT at KPHX may require:
  - Payload reduction or fuel reduction
  - De-rated takeoff thrust
  - Engine performance monitoring for EGT limit proximity

High OAT increases the EGT margin consumed during takeoff. Engines operating
repeatedly at high-OAT high-thrust settings show accelerated HPT blade degradation.
Consistent EGT deviation trending upward at KPHX operations in summer is a leading
indicator tracked via ACARS trend monitoring and evaluated during A-check and
scheduled borescope inspection intervals.
