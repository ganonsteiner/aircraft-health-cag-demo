AIRWORTHINESS DIRECTIVE
Federal Aviation Administration
14 CFR Part 39

AD 2018-23-09: CFM International CFM56-7B Series Turbofan Engines
Fan Blade Root Fatigue Inspection

SUMMARY
This AD requires repetitive inspections of the fan blade roots for fatigue cracking on CFM56-7B engines. Reports of fan blade root cracking have been identified in certain serial number ranges. This condition, if not corrected, could result in fan blade separation and uncontained engine failure.

APPLICABILITY
CFM56-7B series turbofan engines with fan blades having Part Number (P/N) 335-063-801-0 through -0 series, installed on Boeing 737-600, -700, -800, and -900 series aircraft.

COMPLIANCE
Required as indicated, unless already accomplished:

(a) Initial inspection: Within 1,000 flight cycles after the effective date of this AD, perform a fluorescent penetrant inspection (FPI) of all fan blade roots per CFM56-7B ESM Task 72-21-00-200-801.

(b) Repeat inspection interval: Every 3,000 flight cycles thereafter.

(c) If any indication is found during inspection, replace the affected fan blade assembly before further flight per CFM56-7B ESM Task 72-21-00-400-801.

DETAILED INSPECTION PROCEDURE
- Remove fan blades from disk
- Clean blade root dovetail surfaces
- Apply fluorescent penetrant per MIL-PRF-25135 Type I, Method A, Form d
- Inspect under UV light (minimum 1,000 μW/cm²) at 10x magnification
- Reject any linear indication > 1.0 mm or any branching indication

COSTS OF COMPLIANCE
Estimated 8 work-hours per engine for initial inspection. Blade replacement cost: approximately $45,000 per blade set if replacement required.

AUTHORITY
49 USC 106(g), 40113, 44701; 14 CFR 39.19, 39.23.

Effective Date: November 20, 2018
