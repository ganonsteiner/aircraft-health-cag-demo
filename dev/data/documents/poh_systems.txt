BOEING 737-800 NG
FLIGHT CREW OPERATIONS MANUAL — CHAPTER 7: AIRCRAFT AND SYSTEMS DESCRIPTION
Applicable to: CFM56-7B powered 737-800 (Southwest Airlines fleet)

================================================================================
POWERPLANT — CFM56-7B
================================================================================

Engine Model:     CFM International CFM56-7B27E (two engines, underwing)
Type:             High-bypass turbofan, dual-rotor
Bypass Ratio:     5.1:1
Fan Diameter:     61 inches (155 cm)
Thrust (T/O):     27,300 lbf per engine (sea level, ISA, static)
Compressor:
  N1 (fan/LPC):   Single-stage fan + 3-stage booster
  N2 (HPC):       9-stage high-pressure compressor
Combustor:        Annular, low-emissions (CFM TAPS-lite)
Turbine:
  HPT:            1-stage high-pressure turbine
  LPT:            4-stage low-pressure turbine

CFM56-7B Dual-Rotor Architecture:
The fan/LPC spool (N1) and HPC/HPT/LPT spool (N2) operate independently.
N1 vibration (fan imbalance), N2 vibration, and EGT deviation are the primary
health indicators monitored per flight via ACARS. Fan blade fatigue cracks and
HPT stage 1 blade oxidation are the most common cause of unscheduled removals
and are the subject of ADs 2020-14-06 and 2018-23-09 respectively.

FADEC (Full Authority Digital Engine Control):
Each engine has a dual-channel FADEC that controls fuel scheduling, surge
protection, and thrust management. FADEC fault codes are downloaded at each
A-check interval. Persistent N2 core stall codes or EGT spikes may indicate
combustor or HPT degradation.

Engine Oil System:
Oil grade: Mobil Jet Oil II (or approved equivalent)
Pressure normal range: 37–102 psi
Temperature limit: 155 °C
Oil quantity is monitored via cockpit indication. Low oil pressure below 13 psi
at idle triggers an EICAS caution. High oil temperature combined with elevated
metal particle content (from oil analysis) indicates bearing or gear degradation.

================================================================================
FUEL SYSTEM
================================================================================

Configuration: Center tank + two wing tanks
Total usable fuel: ~46,100 lbs (6,875 US gal) typical; varies with interior config
Fuel type: Jet-A / Jet-A1 (Jet-B approved with limitations)
Center tank pumps: two AC motor-driven boost pumps
Wing tank pumps: two AC boost pumps per wing (four total)
Crossfeed: single crossfeed valve allows any pump to feed either engine
Fuel temperature monitoring: main tank temperature probes; minimum -43 °C for
  Jet-A, -47 °C for Jet-A1

================================================================================
HYDRAULIC SYSTEM
================================================================================

Three independent systems: System A, System B, Standby
System A / B pressure: 3,000 psi (normal operating)
Powered by: engine-driven pumps (EDPs) + electric motor-driven pumps (EDP backup)
Functions: flight controls, landing gear, brakes, nose-wheel steering, thrust
  reversers, spoilers
Standby: electric pump; activates automatically for rudder and brakes if A and B fail

================================================================================
FLIGHT CONTROLS
================================================================================

Fly-by-cable primary controls (ailerons, elevator, rudder)
Spoilers: 6 per wing; flight spoilers (roll augmentation) + ground spoilers
Leading edge: slats (Krueger flaps inboard, variable-camber slats outboard)
Trailing edge: single-slotted Fowler flaps (8 positions: 1, 2, 5, 10, 15, 25, 30, 40)

================================================================================
AVIONICS / EFIS
================================================================================

Flight Management System: Honeywell Pegasus (or equivalent per airline STC)
EFIS: dual Captain/FO primary flight displays (PFD) + navigation displays (ND)
EICAS: Engine Indication and Crew Alerting System — two display units
ACARS: Aircraft Communication Addressing and Reporting System; used for
  engine health monitoring data (EGT deviation, N1/N2 vibration) transmission
  to maintenance control after each flight.

================================================================================
LANDING GEAR
================================================================================

Type: Retractable tricycle (nose + two main gear)
Main gear: 2 struts, 4 wheels each (8 main wheels total)
Nose gear: 2 wheels
Braking: carbon brakes (standard on NG), automatic braking system (AUTOBRAKE),
  anti-skid
Typical tire replacement interval: 150–200 cycles (varies with runway conditions)
